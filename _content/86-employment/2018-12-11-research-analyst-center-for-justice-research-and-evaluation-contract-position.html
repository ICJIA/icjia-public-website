The Center for Justice Research and Evaluation conducts applied research and evaluation projects that examine critical criminal and juvenile justice topics and criminal justice program implementation and outcomes in Illinois. The center also conducts presentations and offers technical assistance to help state and local programs and initiatives use data to inform and improve their work. The work supports the state's efforts to use research and evaluation to inform policy and practice. Responsibilities include assisting the manager of the Center for Justice Research and Evaluation by: (1) interpreting and summarizing research methods and findings published in academic journals on topics relevant to the center's primary research focus; (2) developing and administering research methods and protocols, such as surveys and interview guides; (3) analyzing qualitative and quantitative data, using statistical software when appropriate; and (4) assisting with summarizing technical research in written format.
