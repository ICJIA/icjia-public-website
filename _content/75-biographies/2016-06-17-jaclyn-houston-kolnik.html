Jaclyn heads up the Research and Analysis Unit’s newly created center: Center for Victim Studies and works to lead the unit’s efforts in developing a statewide research agenda that will help inform policy, practice, and funding as it relates to victimization and victim services. Jaclyn received her Ph.D. in community psychology from DePaul University, with a focus on violence against women. Her research centers on sexual assault, human trafficking, and intimate partner violence. She has experience completing program evaluations, utilizing various sophisticated statistical methodologies, and translating traditional academic research into tangible action steps for improving policy and practice. Her experience and interests will be a great addition to our agency’s highly skilled and dedicated staff whose work focuses on improving the lives of victims.
