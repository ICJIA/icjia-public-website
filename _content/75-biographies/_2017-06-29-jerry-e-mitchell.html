Jerry E. Mitchell is chief of the Peoria Police Department. Mr. Mitchell has been with the Peoria Police Department for more than 25 years, rising through the ranks from a recruit in 1990 to chief in 2014. Mr. Mitchell has served assignments in Patrol, Vice and Narcotics, Field Training, Information Office, Training, PHA & Taft Homes Walking Beat, and the Support Services Division. He also served as collateral duties supervisor of the Tactical Operation Center, Special Response Team, and Mentoring Program coordinator.

Mr. Mitchell is a member of the Illinois Juvenile Justice Leadership Council. He also served on the Illinois Crisis Negotiators Association Board of Directors.

Mr. Mitchell holds a bachelor’s degree in Administration of Criminal Justice and Sociology from Bradley University. He is a graduate of Northwestern University’s School of Staff and Command.
