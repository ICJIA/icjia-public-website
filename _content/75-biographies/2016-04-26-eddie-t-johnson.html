Superintendent Eddie T. Johnson joined the Chicago Police Department as a patrolman in 1988 and has served in several supervisory roles, including sergeant (5th District), sergeant (Detective Division), and gang/tactical Sergeant. He served as a lieutenant in Patrol in the 15th District (Austin) before being promoted to commander of the 6th District (Gresham), deputy chief for Area Central, executive officer of patrol and finally chief of patrol, where he commanded 8,000 police officers. Mr. Johnson has a received a number of policing awards, including department commendations and the Chicago Defender Men of Excellence Award. He is a member of the Executive Board of NOBLE’s Chicago Chapter, the St. Jude Board of Directors, and the Chicago Police Memorial Foundation Assistance Committee. Mr. Johnson earned a bachelor’s degree at Governors State University. He is expecting his master in public policy and administration degree with a specialization in public safety and national security from Northwestern University in spring 2016.
