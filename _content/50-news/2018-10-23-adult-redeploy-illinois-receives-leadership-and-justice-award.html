The Adult Redeploy Illinois program was honored last week with the Illinois Association of Problem-Solving Courts' 2018 Leadership and Therapeutic Justice Award.

The ILAPSC promotes problem-solving courts in Illinois by providing education, assistance, training, and development through collaboration of behavioral health and justice systems.

The association honors one organization and one individual each year that demonstrate exceptional leadership in promoting and advancing ILAPSC's mission. Adult Redeploy Illinois was honored with the organization award for substantial contributions in implementing and enhancing therapeutic justice responses to persons with behavioral health issues.

The Adult Redeploy Illinois program was honored last week with the Illinois Association of Problem-Solving Courts' 2018 Leadership and Therapeutic Justice Award.

The ILAPSC promotes problem-solving courts in Illinois by providing education, assistance, training, and development through collaboration of behavioral health and justice systems.

The association honors one organization and one individual each year that demonstrate exceptional leadership in promoting and advancing ILAPSC's mission. Adult Redeploy Illinois was honored with the organization award for substantial contributions in implementing and enhancing therapeutic justice responses to persons with behavioral health issues.



From left: ARI Program Director Mary Ann Dyar; ILAPSC President Hon. Jeff Ford; and ILAPSC Individual Leadership Award recipient Michelle Rock, Executive Director of the Illinois Center of Excellence for Behavioral Health & Justice.
