ICJIA GATA Overview

The Grant Accountability and Transparency Act, also known as GATA, was passed by the Illinois Legislature in 2014.

GATA confirms ICJIA's commitment to the Federal Uniform Guidance 2 CFR 200, which went into effect in December 2014. The Uniform Guidance already applies to all federal grants and federal pass-through funds. GATA also requires Illinois' state grants to follow these federal rules for grants which are state funded. This landmark legislations will increase accountability and transparency in the use of grant funds. Once fully implemented, it will unify processes and reduce administrative burdens for state agencies and grantees.

ICJIA Funding by Component

These charts illustrate all funds designated to programs serving the following components of the criminal justice system in Illinois: Corrections, Courts, Defense, ICJIA/Internal, Law Enforcement, Prosecution, Service Providers, Systemwide/General. These charts represent funding designations made during the 18 month period from the 1st Quarter of Calendar Year 2015 through the 2nd Quarter of Calendar Year 2016.

These charts represent funds from federal sources including the Office of Justice Programs (OJP), the Office for Victims of Crime (OVC), and the United States Department of Justice (DOJ).

Federal programs represented include:

  • Justice Assistance Grants (OJP)
  • the Coverdell National Forensic Science Improvement Grants Program (OJP)
  • Project Safe Neighborhoods (OJP)
  • Residential Substance Abuse Treatment for State Prisoners (OJP)
  • Sex Offender Registration and Notification Act (DOJ)
  • Victim Assistance Discretionary Grant Training Program (OVC)
